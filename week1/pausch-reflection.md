# Following your childhood dreams

I loved this lecture and found it very inspirational. One thing that stood out to me was consistent effort and belief despite obstacles. For example, he did not take a rejection letter as an imagineer as final, but he decided to do something about it and use it as an opportunity to show that he really wanted it. One of the time management strategies that he did that resonated with me was choosing what was most valuable. There were many times he had lots of different options about how to proceed in life, but we would always stay focused on his goal and picked the harder path that would help him in the long run. One head fake I have noticed a lot is when a teacher says we are doing a project, building a website or app for example, but then use it as a tool to teach git or scrums or a similar topic. They use something more initially engaging to teach deeper more meaningful topics. I think that headfakes should not always be considered negative, as there are plenty of experiences where you can make something tricky or unintersting on the surface into an engaging and interesting topic. Most people want to build their project, but dont want to do a full class on using VS Code, there are ways to accomplish building the app to be fun while also teaching necessary skills at the same time.
